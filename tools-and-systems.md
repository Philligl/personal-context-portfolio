# Tools and Systems

## Daily Tools

**Gmail** — Primary personal email. Organized with date-based travel labels (format: `YYYY-MM-DD_[trip-name]_[destination]`), current project labels, and long-term business activity labels. Archive workflow: move completed project labels to archive. Spam filtered. Two backup Outlook accounts for business email (read-only, security-locked).

**Google Drive** — Strategic target for consolidation. Planning migration over the next year from Dropbox and OneDrive. All kids use Gmail/Google ecosystem, so standardizing on Google Workspace for family coordination.

**Dropbox** — Years of accumulated work and personal files (Excel, Word, PDF). May retain for cold archive storage but transitioning primary workflow to Google Drive.

**OneDrive** — Multiple accounts:
- Personal account: Family and RFA material, shared with administrative team via personal account (current inefficiency)
- Chamonix account: HOA-related (temporary—will disconnect after board term)
- Planned: Sunset as part of Google Workspace migration

**Excel and Word** — Decades of fluency; primary tools for structured analysis and document creation. Proficient with pivot tables for data analysis. Considering gradual migration to Google Sheets/Docs depending on Claude integration and workflow fit.

**Obsidian** — Used as a reader across three local vaults: Wiki, and two other project-specific Markdown repositories. No plugins. Local storage to avoid sync conflicts with cloud services. Web clipper used frequently to create clean local copies of articles, YouTube transcripts, and other sourced content.

**Perplexity (Comet browser)** — Chrome-based Perplexity browser used to ask Perplexity Assistant for explanations on articles and YouTube videos while reading. Uses "Key Takeaways" feature when content is complex or difficult to absorb quickly.

**NotebookLM** — Used heavily for material curation and output generation. Prefers it over wiki for many use cases. Leverages mindmaps, infographics, and chart outputs.

**Granola** — Meeting capture and transcription tool integrated into workflow.

## Data Sources

**Primary documents** — Dropbox (legacy), OneDrive (personal + Chamonix), Google Drive (migration target)

**Wiki and project-specific content** — Local Obsidian vaults (Markdown)

**Meeting context** — Granola capture

**Web sourced content** — Obsidian web clipper (articles, YouTube transcripts, curated material)

**Curated knowledge base** — NotebookLM (growing preference over wiki for certain material types)

**Analysis and reporting** — Excel pivot tables (no formal databases)

## Integrations and Connections

Minimal automation currently. No cross-tool automation (IFTTT, Zapier, etc.). Email filtering in Gmail for spam. Several manual processes have evolved to the point where they could be standardized and scheduled, but currently all workflows are manual.

## Evaluating or Planning to Adopt

**Google Workspace migration** — Strategic shift from OneDrive/Dropbox over next 12 months. Chosen for security (low IT admin overhead), Gemini integration, and family ecosystem alignment (all kids on Gmail).

**Google Sheets/Docs** — Evaluating adoption depending on Claude integration quality and whether muscle memory can be overcome.

**Hermes Agent** — Planning to experiment as a separate approach to widespread general research. Early-stage interest.

**Data separation** — Republic Finance infrastructure needs separation from personal documents over next 6-12 months due to corporate restructuring.

## Tried and Rejected

**Notion** — Tried multiple times but abandoned. Workflow is isolated (not collaborative), so shared Notion databases add complexity without benefit. Overhead not worth the functionality.

**OpenClaw** — Set up but unused. Broke on every update; reliability issues made it impractical.

**Google Sheets/Docs (tentatively)** — Prefer Excel/Word due to decades of muscle memory, though openness to switching exists pending Claude integration improvements.
