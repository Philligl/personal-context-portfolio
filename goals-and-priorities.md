# Goals and Priorities

## Current Goals

**Implement Addepar reporting infrastructure** — Add Addepar as the reporting tool for all family investments. Success is having a unified, consolidated view that replaces manual one-offs.

**Create an investment reporting and analysis flow** — Once Addepar is in place, build a systematic process for regular reporting and analysis. The goal is ease and frequency, not fundamental strategy change. Better process → better communication with advisors → incremental improvement in investment fit.

**Transition toward family, hobby, and retirement interests** — Deliberately shift focus away from the intensity and problem-solving that has driven your career. Learn to "stop and smell the roses" (acknowledge this is difficult for someone wired to work and accomplish).

**Deepen AI capability for learning** — Get better at AI specifically as a tool to facilitate understanding of things you're interested in, at the intersection of capability and enjoyment. The goal is amplified learning, not outsourcing.

## Longer-Term Goals

**Ongoing rebalancing toward family, travel, and hobbies** — Gradual but consistent shift in how you allocate attention and energy. This is a multi-year reorientation.

**Continuous learning and exploration** — Keep picking up new things. Stay intellectually engaged without the intensity of problem-solving for its own sake.

## How I Think About Tradeoffs

**Default to quality** — You lean strongly toward quality over speed. When you have to choose, quality wins.

**Short-term visibility over long-term optimization** — You prefer to see progress and results in the near term rather than optimize for distant outcomes. This lets you iterate and adjust.

**Growth over stability** — You keep picking up new things. You're not optimizing for stability or consolidation; you're optimizing for new capability and understanding.

**But context matters** — These are instinctive leans, not absolute rules. You apply cost/benefit analysis and adjust based on circumstances.

## What I'm NOT Prioritizing

**Picking better investments** — You're not trying to outperform or chase superior stock picks. You're satisfied with improving fit and alignment within a sound strategy.

**Repetitive manual tasks** — You don't have patience for ongoing, routine manual work anymore. Processes should reduce friction, not add it.

**Guitar (for now)** — This is something you want to pick up again, but it's a time challenge and is deliberately on the back burner.

## What Success Looks Like in Six Months

**More travel** — Actual trips and experiences, especially meaningful given the physical challenges of age 72.

**AI as learning amplifier** — AI has expanded your capacity to understand things you care about. You're using it the way you use the Wiki — as an engagement tool, not a replacement. You learn by interacting, not by consuming.

**Stronger re-engagement with Republic Finance** — You've reconnected with the new majority owner at a level that feels substantive and worth your time.

**Investment process flowing smoothly** — Addepar is in place, reporting happens regularly, analysis is easy to oversee. It requires supervision, not heavy manual labor.

**Noticeable shift toward enjoyment** — Life feels more like learning and experiencing interesting things, less like solving problems for their own sake.
