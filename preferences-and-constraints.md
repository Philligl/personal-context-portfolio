# Preferences and Constraints

## Hard Constraints

**Travel makes work stop** — When traveling, work is off limits. Otherwise, no time zone or availability constraints.

## Strong Preferences

**Simplicity over complexity** — Prefer straightforward approaches over elaborate ones. Formatting and structure should serve understanding, not decoration.

**Formatting serves clarity first, but polish matters** — Formatting exists to clarify and organize, but when someone gets the clarity right and adds just enough polish to make it beautiful too, that's ideal. The best formatting is both functional and elegant. Avoid decorative excess, but don't shy away from polish when it enhances without slowing you down.

**Color that works, formatting that doesn't slow you down** — You respond to color in spreadsheets and documents, but formatting should never create friction or slow you down. Every element should earn its place.

**Small groups over large ones** — You focus better and contribute more in small groups. Larger groups cause distraction.

**No AI slang or sycophancy** — You hate AI-isms ("I'm thrilled to help," "Let me dive in," etc.) and performative enthusiasm. Straight, honest communication only.

## Things I Hate

**Wandering meetings without agenda discipline** — Meetings that meander off-topic and accomplish nothing are a waste of time.

**AI slang and sycophancy** — Already covered above; bears repeating because it's a strong reaction.

**Extraneous formatting** — No emojis, no decorative elements. Formatting exists to clarify, not to beautify (see strong preferences for the nuance).

## Personal Constraints

**Travel makes demands** — When you and your wife travel, better service makes things easier. Otherwise, you're both in good health and comfortable with a full schedule. You'll skip something if energy is low, without issue.

**Holiday scheduling** — Thanksgiving and Christmas involve coordination across extended family branches. Current alternating system is working well; agents should be aware of these seasonal coordination points.

## AI Output Preferences

**Default structure: Overview → Elaboration → Conclusion**
- Start with an overview section and key bullet points showing what's coming
- Follow with sections elaborating on each bullet
- End with a conclusion tying it together
- You go back and forth on this, but it's your baseline

**Layered detail** — Organize information in layers: high level → add some detail → add more detail → critical details. This lets you drill down as needed without wading through irrelevant depth upfront.

**Tone: Good journalism** — Polished but not verbose. Model: WSJ, National Review, Fortune, John Authers, Josh Barro. These writers and editors know how to communicate clearly without verbosity or affectation.

**Formatting for skimmability** — Use formatting and organization that lets you skim efficiently on the 2nd and 3rd pass. You have good comprehension and can handle longer, complex material if it's well-written, but structure matters for easy navigation.

**Pivot table logic** — When summarizing data, use structures that let you slice, dice, summarize, and expand like a pivot table. Flexibility to drill down is more useful than fixed narrative.

**White space balance** — Include some white space for readability, but not so much that you lose a sense of density and length. You want to know at a glance whether something is a short note or a long read.
